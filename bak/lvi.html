Helpful questions to ask your LASIK surgeon

Don't see your question?@SunLaserVision if you've got more questions.

Q: How long has your surgeon been doing LASIK?

Q: Does your surgeon live in this community?

(If your surgeon lives in another city and comes only 1 or 2 days a month to perform surgery, he or she may not be available to help you if a problem arises after surgery.)

Q: Does the surgeon see me during my recovery period?

(A surgeon experience with the natural healing after LASIK who was also the one performing your surgery is most likely to catch any problems early if any should develop.)

Q: What laser do you use to treat my near or farsightedness?

(This will be an excimer laser; for the AMO laser - the most widely used laser platform - the most upgraded level is the Star S4 IR; older platforms are the S3, S2 and S1 model. IR indicates iris registration which has better results for astigmatism. Other manufacturers include the Technolas laser, the Mel 80 from Zeiss, Nidek excimer and the Allegretto/Wavelight from Alcon).

Q: Do you use a microkeratome to make my Lasik flap?

(The microkeratome is older technology originating in the 1950s and uses a blade to create the flap. It is currently the least precise way to make a LASIK flap.)

Q: Do you use a laser to make my lasik flap?

(The most common laser used is the AMO intralase laser - most recent upgraded platform is the IFS, older platforms include the FS60, FS30, FS15. Femtosecond lasers for flap creation significantly reduce the risk of flap related complications over microkeratomes.)

Q: What system do you use to measure my prescription for the laser?

(For the AMO laser, iDesign is the most recent imaging system to measure your eye's optics whereas the Wavescan is the older system. The least precise way to treat for lasik is using your prescription from your glasses. Other manufacturers have optics measurement systems; ensure that the latest system is being used for your eyes.)

Q: Do you use Accutarget HD to ensure the clarity of the eye's optics prior to surgery?

(Accutarget HD is used to ensure that you have no lens or corneal opacities which if not diagnosed may result in vision after surgery that you're not entirely satisfied with.)

Q: What does your price include?

Keep in mind that most laser eye centers charge about $4,000 to $5,000 for bilateral lasik. This will usually cover your preoperative testing, your surgery, your follow up exams for 6 months after surgery as well as any touch if needed within a year of surgery. If the price is discounted, are some of these services not included?
